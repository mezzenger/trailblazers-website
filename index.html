  <section id="science">

    <h2>Science & Innovation</h2>


    <div class="profile">

      <h3>Katherine Johnson</h3>

      <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d7/Katherine_Johnson_1983.jpg" alt="Katherine Johnson">

      <p>Katherine Johnson was a NASA mathematician whose calculations were critical to the success of the first U.S. crewed spaceflights. She was born in 1918 in West Virginia and showed strong mathematical abilities from a young age. She graduated summa cum laude from West Virginia State College at age 18, majoring in mathematics and French. She was married and had three daughters. Her story was featured in the book and film <em>Hidden Figures</em>.</p>

      <p class="attribution">Source: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katherine_Johnson" target="_blank">Wikipedia - Katherine Johnson</a>, Image: NASA (public domain)</p>

    </div>


    <div class="profile">

      <h3>Jane C. Wright</h3>

      <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/49/Jane_C._Wright.jpg" alt="Jane C. Wright">

      <p>Jane C. Wright was a pioneering cancer researcher and physician. She helped develop chemotherapy as a treatment for cancer and was instrumental in discovering how to test drugs on cancer cells. Born in 1919 in New York, she was the daughter of Dr. Louis T. Wright, one of the first African American graduates of Harvard Medical School. She earned her medical degree from New York Medical College. She was married and had two daughters.</p>

      <p class="attribution">Source: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jane_C._Wright" target="_blank">Wikipedia - Jane C. Wright</a>, Image: public domain</p>

    </div>

  </section>


  <section id="government">

    <h2>Government & Politics</h2>


    <div class="profile">

      <h3>Kamala Harris</h3>

      <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e4/Kamala_Harris_official_portrait.jpg" alt="Kamala Harris">

      <p>Kamala Harris is the first woman, the first African American, and the first Asian American Vice President of the United States. Born in Oakland, California, in 1964, her mother was from India and her father from Jamaica. She attended Howard University and earned her law degree from UC Hastings. She has served as a District Attorney, California Attorney General, and U.S. Senator. She is married to attorney Doug Emhoff.</p>

      <p class="attribution">Source: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kamala_Harris" target="_blank">Wikipedia - Kamala Harris</a>, Image: public domain</p>

    </div>


    <div class="profile">

      <h3>Shirley Chisholm</h3>

      <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/6b/Shirley_Chisholm.jpg" alt="Shirley Chisholm">

      <p>Shirley Chisholm was the first African American woman elected to Congress and the first woman to seek the Democratic nomination for U.S. President. Born in 1924 in Brooklyn, New York, she earned a master's degree in education from Columbia University. She worked as a nursery school teacher and educational consultant before entering politics. She was known for her fearless advocacy for minorities and women.</p>

      <p class="attribution">Source: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shirley_Chisholm" target="_blank">Wikipedia - Shirley Chisholm</a>, Image: public domain</p>

    </div>

  </section>


  <section id="arts">

    <h2>Arts, Literature & Media</h2>


    <div class="profile">

      <h3>Toni Morrison</h3>

      <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1c/Toni_Morrison_2008-2.jpg" alt="Toni Morrison">

      <p>Toni Morrison was a Nobel Prize-winning author known for her vivid portrayal of African American life and identity. Born in 1931 in Ohio, she earned degrees from Howard University and Cornell University. She worked as an editor and professor, and authored novels such as <em>Beloved</em> and <em>The Bluest Eye</em>. She had two sons and received numerous honors for her literary work.</p>

      <p class="attribution">Source: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toni_Morrison" target="_blank">Wikipedia - Toni Morrison</a>, Image: public domain</p>

    </div>


    <div class="profile">

      <h3>Oprah Winfrey</h3>

      <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/81/Oprah_in_2014.jpg" alt="Oprah Winfrey">

      <p>Oprah Winfrey is a media mogul, philanthropist, and the first Black woman billionaire in the U.S. Born in Mississippi in 1954, she grew up in poverty and began working in radio and television in her teens. She studied at Tennessee State University. Her talk show became one of the most influential in history. Oprah has also founded her own television network and supported numerous charitable causes.</p>

      <p class="attribution">Source: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey" target="_blank">Wikipedia - Oprah Winfrey</a>, Image: public domain</p>

    </div>

  </section>


  <section id="sports">

    <h2>Sports</h2>


    <div class="profile">

      <h3>Wilma Rudolph</h3>

      <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/38/Wilma_Rudolph_1960.jpg" alt="Wilma Rudolph">

      <p>Wilma Rudolph was the first American woman to win three gold medals in a single Olympic Games (1960). Born in 1940 in Tennessee, she overcame polio as a child and began running competitively in high school. She attended Tennessee State University. Her Olympic success made her an international icon and a symbol of perseverance.</p>

      <p class="attribution">Source: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilma_Rudolph" target="_blank">Wikipedia - Wilma Rudolph</a>, Image: public domain</p>

    </div>


    <div class="profile">

      <h3>Serena Williams</h3>

      <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b9/Serena_Williams_2013_US_Open.jpg" alt="Serena Williams">

      <p>Serena Williams is considered one of the greatest tennis players of all time. Born in 1981 in Michigan, she was coached by her father alongside her sister Venus. She has won 23 Grand Slam singles titles and numerous Olympic medals. Serena is also a fashion designer, philanthropist, and mother.</p>

      <p class="attribution">Source: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serena_Williams" target="_blank">Wikipedia - Serena Williams</a>, Image: public domain</p>

    </div>

  </section>


  <section id="business">

    <h2>Business & Innovation</h2>


    <div class="profile">

      <h3>Madam C.J. Walker</h3>

      <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/68/Madam_CJ_Walker_face_circa_1914.jpg" alt="Madam C.J. Walker">

      <p>Madam C.J. Walker was the first female self-made millionaire in America. Born in 1867 to formerly enslaved parents, she developed a successful line of beauty and hair products for African American women. She established a business empire and trained thousands of women as sales agents. She was also a philanthropist and social activist.</p>

      <p class="attribution">Source: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madam_C._J._Walker" target="_blank">Wikipedia - Madam C. J. Walker</a>, Image: public domain</p>

    </div>


    <div class="profile">

      <h3>Ursula Burns</h3>

      <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1b/Ursula_Burns_2011.jpg" alt="Ursula Burns">

      <p>Ursula Burns was the first Black woman to become CEO of a Fortune 500 company (Xerox). Born in 1958 in New York City, she was raised by a single mother in public housing. She earned degrees in mechanical engineering from Polytechnic Institute and Columbia University. She became a powerful advocate for education and inclusion in business.</p>

      <p class="attribution">Source: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ursula_Burns" target="_blank">Wikipedia - Ursula Burns</a>, Image: public domain</p>
